TrueSkill (TS)

TrueSkill is designed to estimate the skill level of players in multiplayer games. It uses a probabilistic model to predict the outcome of a match and updates player ratings after each game.

Mu (μ)

Represents the estimated skill of a player. A higher μ generally indicates a better player. When a player wins a game, μ increases, and when they lose, μ decreases. The magnitude of this change depends on the expected outcome and the uncertainty (σ).

Sigma (σ)

Denotes the uncertainty in a player's skill estimation. A new player will have a high σ because the system is unsure of their skill. As a player plays more games, σ decreases, signifying increased confidence in the μ estimation.

Adjusted TrueSkill

Adjusted TS = μ - (2 × σ). There is also a pick bonus applied to a players μ, so that players who get picked earlier get a rating boost. This is only so that players with a very high winrate, but lower average pick, do not get very high ratings. There is also a decay factor added, so that more recent games have a larger impact on player rating.

Win Chance

This is derived by comparing the Adjusted TrueSkill values of players in each team. The win chance is determined using the average skill ratings (with the formula Adjusted TS = μ - 2σ) of the players in each team.
